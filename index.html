Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur and economist, best known for pioneering the concept of microfinance. He founded Grameen Bank in 1983, offering small loans to impoverished people, especially women, without requiring collateral. This model has empowered millions, providing a pathway out of poverty. Yunus’s work has earned global recognition, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006. His vision of social business—enterprises focused on addressing social issues rather than profit maximization—has inspired numerous initiatives worldwide. Yunus continues to advocate for financial inclusion, social entrepreneurship, and economic empowerment to reduce poverty and inequality across the globe.
